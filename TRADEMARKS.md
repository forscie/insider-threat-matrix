## Proper Use

Use the full name **Insider Threat Matrix™** on first reference in public
materials, including documentation, websites, repositories, publications,
presentations, training materials, and product materials.

After first reference, **ITM** may be used where the context is clear.

Do not alter the name, stylise it inconsistently, or use it in a way that
may cause confusion about ownership, source, affiliation, or endorsement.

Use of the Insider Threat Matrix™ name, framework, taxonomy,
identifiers, content, detections, preventions, mappings, or related
materials does not imply sponsorship, endorsement, affiliation,
certification, approval, or control by Forscie Limited.

You must not state or imply that Forscie Limited sponsors, endorses,
approves, certifies, or is affiliated with your product, service,
organisation, publication, training, integration, or implementation
unless Forscie Limited has expressly agreed to that use in writing.

## Product, Repository, and Domain Names

Do not use the Insider Threat Matrix™ name, ITM name, or any confusingly
similar name as part of a product name, company name, service name,
certification name, package name, repository name, domain name, or social
media account name in a way that may cause confusion about ownership,
source, affiliation, or endorsement.

Descriptive references are permitted where they are truthful, limited,
and do not imply endorsement or affiliation.

Acceptable examples include:

- "Compatible with the Insider Threat Matrix™"
- "Mapping to Insider Threat Matrix™ entries"
- "Detection content aligned to the Insider Threat Matrix™"

Unacceptable examples include:

- "Official Insider Threat Matrix™ Platform"
- "Forscie-approved ITM Integration"
- "Certified Insider Threat Matrix™ Partner"
- "Insider Threat Matrix™ Detection Suite"

unless Forscie Limited has expressly authorised that use in writing.

Suitable locations include a NOTICE file, repository README,
documentation page, attribution page, website footer, software about

## Framework Neutrality

The Insider Threat Matrix™ is a vendor-neutral, practitioner-led
investigative framework.

Sponsorship, contribution, integration, mapping, implementation, or other
use of the framework does not grant editorial control over the framework
and does not constitute endorsement of any product, service, vendor, or
organisation.

Sponsorship supports development and public availability but does not
shape the framework or its content.
